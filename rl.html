Staff AI Research Scientist

Variant B — Reinforcement learning · Santa Clara, CA · Variant A (verifiable optimization)

The Role

We are hiring a highly technical and creative AI Research Scientist to join our AI team. You will investigate and advance the state of the art in reinforcement learning for generative AI—developing and scaling post-training methods that improve model capability, alignment, and task performance on challenging engineering workloads. You will research novel RL algorithms and training pipelines, run rigorous experiments at scale, and help translate advances into production-ready model improvements.

The Person

You are motivated by fundamental RL problems and their application to large language and generative models. You think carefully about reward design, policy optimization, training stability, and evaluation of RL-trained models. You enjoy building end-to-end RL pipelines—from data and reward modeling through distributed training and ablation studies—and collaborating with infrastructure and product teams to land results. You communicate clearly in papers, talks, and demos and stay current with fast-moving developments in RL for LLMs.

Key Responsibilities

  • Research and develop reinforcement learning methods for post-training large language and generative models on engineering tasks
  • Design reward functions, preference models, and evaluation protocols that reflect task quality and production requirements
  • Implement and scale RL training pipelines (for example: policy-gradient methods, preference optimization, and hybrid SFT–RL workflows) on large GPU clusters
  • Run controlled experiments to understand sample efficiency, training dynamics, and generalization of RL-trained models
  • Partner with engineering teams to integrate RL post-training into model development workflows and measure impact on downstream tasks
  • Publish and present findings at top-tier venues and contribute to internal knowledge sharing around RL for generative AI

Preferred Experience

  • Track record of publications at top-tier venues (for example: NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, AAAI, or similar)
  • Demonstrated experience with reinforcement learning for language or generative models—for example: RLHF, RLAIF, or policy optimization for LLMs
  • Hands-on experience with RL algorithms and frameworks (for example: PPO, GRPO, DPO, or related methods)
  • Experience training large models in distributed GPU environments
  • Experience designing rewards, preference data collection, or evaluation for aligned model behavior
  • Demonstrated experience applying LLMs to complex technical or engineering tasks
  • Proven ability to communicate complex technical ideas through papers, presentations, demos, and written content

Academic Credentials

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or related field.
  • PhD in Computer Science, Machine Learning, or a related field strongly preferred. Master's with an exceptional publication record in reinforcement learning, generative AI, or applied machine learning will be considered.

Outcomes

Success in this role is measured by measurable gains from RL post-training on production-relevant tasks:

  • Model capability — improve task success rates by 30% on held-out engineering benchmarks through RL post-training
  • Training efficiency — reduce RL training cost or wall-clock time by 25% while maintaining or improving model quality
  • Production integration — ship at least one RL post-training pipeline into the model development workflow with documented evaluation and rollback criteria
