Staff AI Research Scientist

Variant C — Multimodal hardware & vision · Santa Clara, CA · Variant A · Variant B

The Role

We are hiring a highly technical and creative AI Research Scientist to join our AI team. You will investigate and advance the state of the art in multimodal AI for hardware, with a focus on computer vision and vision-language methods applied to AMD's hardware generation process—reasoning over layouts, floorplans, inspection imagery, engineering diagrams, and combined visual and textual design artifacts. You will define verifiable multimodal tasks, develop novel models through rigorous experimentation, and help translate research into practical capabilities across design, verification, and manufacturing workflows.

The Person

You are driven by problems that require connecting visual information to engineering decisions—understanding what is in an image or diagram and what it means for hardware quality, correctness, or manufacturability. You enjoy building multimodal systems that combine computer vision with language, structured metadata, and domain knowledge. You care about verifiable evaluation on real hardware artifacts, collaborate effectively with design, verification, and manufacturing teams, and communicate clearly in papers, talks, and demos. You stay current with fast-moving developments in vision-language models and multimodal learning.

Key Responsibilities

  • Define verifiable multimodal tasks for AMD's hardware generation process—combining computer vision with engineering data and explicit success criteria tied to hardware-relevant outcomes
  • Research and develop vision-language and multimodal models for hardware artifacts (for example: layout and floorplan images, inspection and metrology imagery, schematics, engineering diagrams, and paired visual-textual documentation)
  • Build datasets, benchmarks, and evaluation harnesses for hardware-centric vision and multimodal tasks; run controlled comparisons and confirm that reported gains are consistent and auditable
  • Integrate multimodal capabilities into design automation, verification, and manufacturing inspection workflows in collaboration with domain experts
  • Conduct original research on modern multimodal methods (for example: vision-language pretraining, fine-grained visual reasoning, document and layout understanding, and multimodal agents) applied to hardware engineering tasks
  • Publish and present findings at top-tier venues and contribute to internal knowledge sharing around computer vision and multimodal AI for hardware

Preferred Experience

  • Track record of publications at top-tier venues (for example: NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, AAAI, CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, or similar)—with strong preference for computer vision and multimodal work
  • Demonstrated experience applying computer vision or multimodal models to engineering, manufacturing, inspection, or hardware-adjacent domains
  • Experience with vision-language models, visual document understanding, layout analysis, anomaly detection, or fine-grained visual reasoning
  • Demonstrated experience training, fine-tuning, or evaluating multimodal and vision models on domain-specific data at meaningful scale
  • Experience working in cross-functional research teams alongside hardware design, verification, manufacturing, or automation engineers
  • Proven ability to communicate complex technical ideas through papers, presentations, demos, and written content
  • Contributions to open-source research codebases or reproducible research artifacts, especially in computer vision, multimodal learning, or AI for hardware

Academic Credentials

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or related field.
  • PhD in Computer Science, Machine Learning, Electrical Engineering, or a related field strongly preferred. Master's with an exceptional publication record in computer vision, multimodal learning, or AI for hardware engineering will be considered.

Outcomes

Success in this role is measured against concrete targets across verifiable multimodal hardware tasks:

  • Visual inspection and anomaly detection — improve defect and anomaly detection accuracy on manufacturing and pre-silicon visual artifacts by 40%
  • Multimodal design understanding — reduce expert review time on combined visual and textual hardware data by 50% through automated multimodal reasoning
  • Layout and floorplan analysis — improve automated extraction and verification of design constraints from visual layout representations by 30%
