Staff AI Research Scientist

Variant A — Verifiable optimization · Santa Clara, CA · Variant B (reinforcement learning)

The Role

We are hiring a highly technical and creative AI Research Scientist to join our AI team. You will investigate and advance the state of the art in generative AI, with a focus on verifiable optimization tasks for complex engineering workflows—defining measurable objectives, improving automation pipelines, and optimizing outcomes against metrics that matter for production. You will develop novel approaches through rigorous experimentation and help translate research into reproducible, real-world gains.

The Person

You are driven by open research questions and enjoy designing experiments that yield measurable advances. You are energized by turning ambiguous engineering problems into verifiable tasks—with clear inputs, success criteria, and reproducible evaluation on real workloads. You care about outcomes such as performance, efficiency, quality, and turnaround time. You articulate complex AI ideas clearly in papers, talks, and demos, collaborate effectively across research and engineering teams, and stay current with fast-moving developments in generative AI.

Key Responsibilities

  • Define verifiable optimization tasks with explicit success criteria tied to engineering outcomes that matter for production
  • Research and develop AI methods that measurably improve performance on those tasks across automation pipelines and large-scale engineering systems
  • Build reproducible benchmarks and evaluation harnesses; run controlled before/after comparisons on held-out workloads and confirm that reported gains are consistent and auditable
  • Conduct original research on modern AI methods (for example: reasoning post-training, reinforcement learning, and new generative model techniques) applied to verifiable optimization problems
  • Publish and present findings at top-tier venues and contribute to internal knowledge sharing around applied generative AI for engineering

Preferred Experience

  • Track record of publications at top-tier venues (for example: NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, AAAI, or similar)
  • Demonstrated experience defining or executing verifiable optimization tasks in engineering workflows
  • Demonstrated experience applying machine learning to automate or improve outcomes in large-scale technical systems
  • Experience working in cross-functional research teams alongside domain experts and engineering partners
  • Demonstrated experience training and applying LLMs to challenging engineering tasks
  • Proven ability to communicate complex technical ideas through papers, presentations, demos, and written content
  • Contributions to open-source research codebases or reproducible research artifacts

Academic Credentials

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or related field.
  • PhD in Computer Science, Machine Learning, or a related field strongly preferred. Master's with an exceptional publication record in generative AI or applied machine learning will be considered.

Outcomes

Success in this role is measured against clear optimization targets on verifiable engineering tasks:

  • Core design optimization — improve key performance metrics by 40% while reducing manual effort by 50%
  • Change and fix workflows — improve coverage by 50% and shorten turnaround on complex tasks by months
  • Systems optimization — achieve 25% improvement on optimization metrics with substantially less human involvement
  • Runtime optimization — achieve 20% latency improvement on AI workloads
