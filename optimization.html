Staff AI Research Scientist

Variant A — Verifiable optimization · Santa Clara, CA · Variant B · Variant C

The Role

We are hiring a highly technical and creative AI Research Scientist to join our AI team. You will investigate and advance the state of the art in generative AI, with a focus on verifiable code optimization tasks for AMD's hardware generation process—defining measurable objectives, improving design automation pipelines, and optimizing outcomes against manufacturing-critical metrics. You will develop novel approaches through rigorous experimentation and help translate research into reproducible gains across AMD's pre-silicon development process.

The Person

You are driven by open research questions and enjoy designing experiments that yield measurable advances. You are energized by turning ambiguous engineering problems into verifiable tasks—with clear inputs, success criteria, and reproducible evaluation on real hardware generation workloads. You care about manufacturing-critical outcomes such as power, performance, area, timing closure, routability, and yield readiness. You articulate complex AI ideas clearly in papers, talks, and demos, collaborate effectively with research, design, and automation teams, and stay current with fast-moving developments in generative AI.

Key Responsibilities

  • Define verifiable optimization tasks for AMD's hardware generation process, with explicit success criteria tied to manufacturing-critical metrics (for example: power, performance, area, timing closure, routability, and yield readiness)
  • Research and develop code optimizations that measurably improve performance on those tasks across design automation pipelines, synthesis and implementation flows, and verification-oriented engineering workflows
  • Build reproducible benchmarks and evaluation harnesses; run controlled before/after comparisons on held-out design workloads and confirm that reported gains are consistent and auditable
  • Conduct original research on modern AI methods (for example: reasoning post-training, reinforcement learning for code and engineering tasks, and new generative model techniques) applied to these verifiable hardware-generation tasks
  • Publish and present findings at top-tier venues and contribute to internal knowledge sharing around AI for design automation and hardware engineering code optimization

Preferred Experience

  • Track record of publications at top-tier venues (for example: NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, AAAI, CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, or similar)
  • Demonstrated experience defining or executing verifiable optimization tasks in hardware design workflows, with evaluation tied to manufacturing-critical metrics
  • Demonstrated experience optimizing or automating code in hardware generation workflows—for example: design automation pipelines, synthesis and implementation flows, verification tooling, or large-scale pre-silicon engineering codebases
  • Experience working in cross-functional research teams alongside hardware design, verification, or automation engineers
  • Demonstrated experience training and applying LLMs and code models to challenging engineering tasks, including reasoning over large codebases and iterative refinement workflows
  • Proven ability to communicate complex technical ideas through papers, presentations, demos, and written content
  • Contributions to open-source research codebases or reproducible research artifacts, especially in AI for code, design automation, or hardware engineering

Academic Credentials

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, or related field.
  • PhD in Computer Science, Machine Learning, Electrical Engineering, or a related field strongly preferred. Master's with an exceptional publication record in generative AI, design automation, EDA, computer architecture, or AI for hardware engineering will be considered.

Outcomes

Success in this role is measured against concrete optimization targets across verifiable hardware-generation tasks:

  • RTL optimization — improve PPA metrics by 40% while reducing the need for human code generation by 50%
  • ECO fixing — improve coverage by 50% while shortening ECO fixing timelines by months on complex tasks
  • CPU code optimization — achieve 25% improvement on optimization metrics while substantially reducing reliance on human code generation
  • GPU kernel optimization — achieve 20% latency improvement on AI workloads running on AI hardware
